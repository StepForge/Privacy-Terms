Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy (“Policy”) explains how StepForge (“StepForge”, “we”, “us”, or “the App”) processes information when you install or use the App. StepForge is developed and maintained by Iman Mirzazadegan (“Developer”).

StepForge is designed to be practical and privacy-respecting. Most of your activity data is kept on your device. Some optional features (such as Health Connect integration and cloud backup/restore) may involve third-party services. This Policy explains what that means in plain language and in detail.

1. Scope

This Policy applies to StepForge distributed through official channels (such as Google Play) and covers the features available within the App, including step tracking, goals, history, widgets, notifications, water reminders and intake, sleep tracking, App Lock, Health Connect integration, feedback/support flows, and Sync & Backup.

2. Definitions

To make this Policy easier to follow, we use the terms below:

  • “Device data” means data stored locally on your phone/tablet (Room database, DataStore, app files).
  • “Health Connect” means Google’s Health Connect platform that stores health data and manages app permissions.
  • “Cloud backup” means optional storage of supported StepForge data in a cloud database (via Firebase services).
  • “Personal data” means information that can identify you directly or indirectly (definitions vary by region).

3. Summary (privacy at a glance)

Default behavior

StepForge stores most of your data on your device. Your daily steps, history, hydration logs, sleep logs, and settings are stored locally so the app works without requiring an account.

Optional features

If you choose to enable them, StepForge can (a) read permitted health records from Health Connect and (b) perform cloud backup/restore via Firebase. You can use StepForge without enabling either option.

Important: StepForge does not sell your personal data. StepForge does not embed advertising SDKs designed to track you across apps. If you use cloud backup, your backup is stored in cloud infrastructure to support restore.

4. Data we process

4.1 Steps and activity data (device sensors)

StepForge can read step counts from your device’s motion sensors (for example the step counter sensor). StepForge may store your step totals to provide history, widgets, and progress visuals.

  • Daily step totals (by date)
  • Optional hourly step snapshots (by date/hour) to enable hourly charts
  • Derived estimates shown in the UI (for example distance and calories estimates)

Derived estimates are approximate and depend on device behavior and optional profile settings.

4.2 History and daily summaries

StepForge includes a History view and a day detail screen. These screens may show daily steps and additional daily context such as water intake and sleep sessions if you use those features. These values are stored locally to make history fast and available offline.

4.3 Hydration and Water Reminder

StepForge may let you set a daily hydration goal, record how much water you drank, and schedule hydration reminders.

  • Daily water totals (ml) per date
  • Reminder settings (enabled/disabled, interval, active hours)
  • Local “today” counters stored for convenience

4.4 Sleep tracking

StepForge includes sleep tracking. Sleep sessions may be logged manually or imported from Health Connect if you grant permission. StepForge stores sleep sessions locally for your history and insights screens.

  • Sleep session start/end times, total duration, and a quality score
  • Sleep source label (for example “manual” or “health_connect”)
  • Optional sleep stages (when available from Health Connect)

4.5 Profile data you provide

StepForge can store optional profile fields if you choose to enter them. These fields can improve the UI experience and help estimate distance/calories.

  • Name/nickname
  • Gender, birth date (used to derive age), height, weight
  • Preferred units (km/mi)

4.6 App preferences and settings

StepForge stores app settings to provide a consistent experience, such as:

  • Daily step goal
  • Theme mode (dark/light/system)
  • Reminder time
  • Auto-sync/backup toggles

4.7 Widgets

StepForge offers home screen widgets (multiple sizes). Widgets display your step totals and progress. To do this, StepForge stores the latest step totals locally and updates widgets via Android mechanisms.

4.8 Notifications and reminders

StepForge can schedule local notifications to remind you to move, to log water, or to show goal achievements. Notifications are created and delivered on your device. You can disable notifications at any time in Android settings.

4.9 Health Connect data (optional)

If you connect StepForge with Health Connect, StepForge may read the data types you explicitly grant, such as:

  • Steps
  • Distance
  • Total calories burned
  • Weight
  • Sleep sessions

StepForge uses Health Connect data to unify your totals across sources, show consistent history, and reduce double counting when both sensor-based and Health Connect data are present.

4.10 App Lock and security recovery data

If you enable App Lock, StepForge may store locally:

  • Whether App Lock is enabled
  • A 4-digit PIN (to unlock StepForge)
  • Whether biometric unlock is allowed (biometric data itself is handled by Android)
  • Lock timeout behavior
  • Security questions/answers if you set them for PIN recovery

Note: StepForge does not receive or store your fingerprint/face data. Biometric authentication is performed by your device’s biometric system.

4.11 Cloud backup and restore (optional)

StepForge may offer Sync & Backup features that upload supported data to cloud storage using Firebase services. The goal is to allow restore on reinstall or device change.

Backups are designed to include supported categories such as:

  • Daily step history
  • Selected non-sensitive settings (for example unit, daily goal, theme, reminder time, auto-sync toggle)

Backups are designed to avoid sensitive fields (for example personal profile details and App Lock secrets) unless explicitly needed and clearly disclosed.

4.12 Crash diagnostics and stability reports

StepForge may use crash reporting (for example Firebase Crashlytics) to understand crashes and improve stability. Crash reports generally include device/app metadata (like OS version, device model) and an error trace. StepForge does not intentionally include your step-by-step history or health records in crash reports.

5. How we use data

We process the data described above for purposes such as:

  • Core tracking: show daily step totals, progress, and history
  • Personalization: apply theme, unit, and goal preferences
  • Insights: provide daily summary screens and optional sleep/hydration context
  • Reminders: schedule daily reminders, hydration reminders, and activity prompts
  • Security: enable App Lock and manage unlock sessions
  • Optional integrations: read Health Connect data when permission is granted
  • Optional cloud features: backup/restore supported data
  • Reliability: diagnose crashes and improve performance

6. Where data is stored

6.1 On your device (default)

Most StepForge data is stored on your device in the app’s private storage. This includes Room databases and DataStore preferences. This storage is not accessible by other apps under normal Android security rules.

6.2 Health Connect (optional)

If you enable Health Connect, health records may be stored in Health Connect. StepForge can read only what you allow. You can review permissions in Health Connect settings.

6.3 Cloud storage for backups (optional)

If you use Sync & Backup, StepForge can upload a backup to cloud infrastructure (Firebase). Backup data is used for restore and continuity, not for advertising.

7. Sharing & third parties

StepForge does not sell your personal data. StepForge may share limited data only when necessary to provide features you request. Examples include:

  • Health Connect (Google): to read the health data types you permit.
  • Firebase services (Google): for authentication (if used), cloud backup storage, and crash diagnostics.
  • Your email app: when you choose to send feedback, StepForge opens your email client to send a message.

Third-party services are governed by their own policies. StepForge cannot control third-party service availability.

8. Permissions & device controls

StepForge may request Android permissions to enable features. Examples include:

  • Activity Recognition (to count steps)
  • Notifications (to show reminders)
  • Exact alarms (to schedule reminders reliably on some Android versions)
  • Health Connect permissions (only for data types you enable)

You can manage permissions in Android Settings and Health Connect Settings at any time.

9. Retention, deletion, and “Clear Data”

9.1 Local data deletion

StepForge stores most data locally. You can remove local StepForge data by:

  • Using in-app options such as clearing history (where available), or
  • Using Privacy & Security → Clear Local Data, or
  • Uninstalling StepForge (removes StepForge’s app storage from your device).

9.2 Cloud backup deletion

If you used Sync & Backup, a copy of supported data may exist in the cloud as your “latest” backup.

Planned improvement: We are preparing an in-app option to delete cloud backup data directly (for example as part of an enhanced “Clear Data” flow). Once available, it will allow you to remove your stored backups from the cloud in addition to clearing local data.

Until that option is available, you can generally reduce what remains in the cloud by overwriting with a newer backup, disconnecting the linked account, and/or contacting us for assistance.

9.3 Health Connect deletion

Health Connect data is managed within Health Connect. If you want to delete health records stored there, use the Health Connect app/system controls. StepForge cannot delete Health Connect data unless you explicitly initiate such an operation through Health Connect-supported actions and permissions.

10. Security

We take reasonable measures to protect StepForge data and reduce the amount of data processed. Security measures include:

  • Keeping most data on-device by default
  • Using Android app sandboxing and recommended libraries (Room/DataStore)
  • Offering an optional App Lock (PIN + optional biometrics)
  • Using secure transport for cloud features (HTTPS/TLS)

No system can guarantee absolute security. For best protection, keep your Android device updated and use a device screen lock.

11. Children’s privacy

StepForge is not designed specifically for children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and want data removed, you can clear StepForge’s local data or uninstall the app. If a cloud backup exists and you need assistance, you may contact the Developer.

12. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy to reflect new features, security improvements, or legal requirements. When changes are significant, the updated Policy will be published on this page and may be referenced inside the app.
