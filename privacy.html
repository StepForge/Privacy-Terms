Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how StepFit (“the App”) handles your information. StepFit is designed and developed by Iman Mirzazadegan as a personal pedometer and fitness companion. The App is intentionally built to minimize the amount of personal data collected and to keep your information primarily on your own device.

1. Scope and role

This Policy applies to all versions of StepFit distributed through official app stores (such as Google Play) and covers all features available within the App, including step tracking, goals, history, widgets, notifications, and Health Connect integration.

StepFit does not operate its own cloud backend. In normal use, your data flows between:

  • your device sensors and the App,
  • the App and Health Connect (if you enable it),
  • local storage on your phone or tablet.

2. Data we collect

2.1 Step and activity data

StepFit uses your device’s motion sensors (for example, the hardware step counter) and, if enabled, Health Connect to read daily step counts and related metrics. This may include:

  • step count and timestamps,
  • derived distance estimates,
  • derived calorie estimates (if available),
  • widgets showing daily totals.

These values are stored locally on your device and used only inside the App to:

  • display your current progress,
  • build your daily/weekly/monthly history,
  • trigger local goal notifications or reminders.

We do not send your step data to our own servers. Any sync with cloud services (for example backups via your OS) is controlled by your device and not by StepFit directly.

2.2 Profile and configuration data

StepFit may optionally store data you provide, such as:

  • name or nickname,
  • age, gender, height, weight,
  • preferred units (kilometers or miles),
  • daily step goal, notification time and theme preferences.

This information is used purely to personalize your experience, including:

  • more accurate distance or calorie approximations,
  • goal‑related visuals and progress calculations,
  • choosing when reminders should be shown.

Profile and configuration data are stored using Android’s secure local storage mechanisms (DataStore and Room). StepFit does not transmit this information to any remote server owned by the developer.

2.3 Health Connect data

When you choose to connect StepFit with Health Connect, the App may read the data types that you explicitly grant access to, such as:

  • Steps
  • Distance
  • Total calories burned
  • Weight

StepFit uses this information to:

  • import steps from other apps and devices into your StepFit view,
  • keep your local totals close to your consolidated health records,
  • avoid double counting when both the sensor and Health Connect are active.

StepFit requests only the permissions it needs for the features you enable. You remain in full control and can revoke these permissions at any time from Health Connect’s settings.

2.4 Diagnostic information

The App may collect anonymous, aggregated information such as crash reports or basic performance metrics through standard platform tools. These are used solely to improve stability and user experience and do not include your personal profile, health data or step‑by‑step history.

3. Notifications and reminders

StepFit can schedule local notifications to remind you to move, drink water, or highlight that you have reached your daily step goal. These notifications are created and handled entirely on your device.

You can disable or adjust notifications at any time from the in‑app Settings or via the standard notification controls of your operating system.

4. Data retention and deletion

Because StepFit stores data primarily on your device:

  • You can reset today’s steps, clear local history or adjust your profile directly from the App’s settings.
  • Uninstalling the App removes all StepFit data stored on the device.

If you also use Health Connect, data stored in Health Connect can be managed and removed via the Health Connect app or system settings, and is subject to Google’s privacy policies.

5. Security

StepFit relies on the security of the underlying mobile platform and on well‑tested Android libraries. While no mobile app or system can ever be 100% secure, StepFit follows these principles:

  • limiting permissions to what is necessary for step counting, notifications and optional integrations;
  • keeping user data local whenever possible, instead of central cloud storage;
  • not embedding invasive advertising SDKs that secretly track your behavior.

6. Children’s privacy

StepFit is not specifically targeted at children under 13 years of age. If you are a parent or guardian and believe that a child is using StepFit with personal information you do not wish to be stored, you can remove that data by clearing the App’s data or uninstalling it on the child’s device.

7. Changes to this Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated to reflect new features, changes in applicable laws, or improvements in how data is handled. When a significant change occurs, a new version of the Policy will be published together with the App update.

Your continued use of StepFit after these changes become effective will be interpreted as acceptance of the updated Policy.
